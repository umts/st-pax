Policies

  • Passengers are allowed to bring two (2) reasonably sized, easily controlled packages on board. Large items, such as skis, carriages, bikes, etc. will not be permitted on the vans.
  • Passengers are allowed 2 friends per ride as long as they are going to the same destination as the passenger.
  • In the unlikely event of an accident, please remain calm. If you are injured, please notify the driver immediately. SpecTrans policy requires that the driver remain at the scene until the police and a staff member have arrived and cleared the scene. We will not be able to bring you to your destination until another van can be sent out to continue the ride.
  • Animals are not allowed aboard the vans, with the exception of certified service animals.
  • Our lifts are an extremely safe way to board or exit a van, but proper lift use requires cooperation between the driver and the passenger. Once the lift is ready for you to board, you should position yourself on the lift with your back facing the van. Please lock your wheels or turn the power off to prevent any movement of the chair while the lift is in motion.
  • As a passenger in SpecTrans vans you are required, by Massachusetts State Law: MGL Part 1, Title XIV, Chapter 90, Section 13A, to have your seat belt fastened at all times while the vehicle is in motion.
  • Rides from an off-campus location to another off-campus locations are limited to two per week, after 5PM.
  • Van service is provided whenever possible during inclement weather. In the event of a snow or ice storm, however, we will discourage people from using our service unless absolutely necessary. Inclement weather requires that we all work together, so please, if the University delays or cancels classes, SpecTrans will assume all rides are cancelled unless you call to reschedule.
  • Alcohol is not permitted on the vans under any circumstances. If you try to get on a van with any alcoholic substance, the driver will refuse the ride.

Scheduling Rides

SpecTrans vans travel on campus and in close proximity to the University, we do not travel more than 3 miles from Du Bois Library. 

  • When you call to schedule rides, you will speak to either the Dispatcher (during the weekday) or the Bus Radio Operator (nights and weekends). Keep in mind that the Dispatcher is more knowledgeable about the service, so you reserve your more complicated questions for weekdays.
  • Permanent rides are those which you will need every week for the entire semester. You may schedule permanent rides during the first two weeks of the semester, and you may update them at any time. If you don’t need a permanent ride on a particular day, please call to cancel ahead of time.
  • During Finals Week, all permanent rides for student passengers will end on the last day of classes, so if you have permanent rides scheduled that are not class related, you will have to call and reschedule (this includes trips to and from dining halls, work, etc.). If you are solely a staff or faculty member of the University, rides to and from work, and work only, will remain scheduled as usual.
  • Following some holidays during the semester, the University will designate that a certain day of the week will follow another day’s class schedule. On such days, our schedules will be adjusted accordingly.
  • There is generally one pick-up/drop-off spot per building. If you are unsure where the spot is for a building, please ask the dispatcher when you schedule the ride. DO NOT ask drivers to drop you off anywhere else. If you need special accommodations, please email the ST Supervisors at stspv1@gmail.com
  • A no-show is a ride that you miss completely, are more than five minutes late for, or that you cancel with less than two hours notice. Passengers who accrue four (4) or more no-shows will be suspended from the service for one week. If you ‘No-Show’ to a ride and do not call, all of your rides will be canceled for the rest of the day.
  • A lateness is a ride for which you are more than 3 minutes late. Passengers who are late eight (8) or more times in a thirty day period, may be suspended from the service for one week. Van will wait exactly 5 minutes before they move on.
  • If your driver is not there within 3 minutes, call the Dispatcher at 413-545-2086.
